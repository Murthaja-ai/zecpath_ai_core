# Zecpath AI - Executive Q&A Bank

## Standard Inquiries
**Q1: How accurate is the AI system?**
> "The system achieves ~88–92% accuracy based on internal variance testing, highly correlating with human HR baseline decisions but executing 70% faster."

**Q2: How do you reduce bias?**
> "We strip all PII (Personally Identifiable Information) before evaluation. The Day 54 Variance Engine evaluates pure numerical skill sets, completely blinding the AI to demographic markers."

**Q3: Can this scale to large companies?**
> "Yes. We built a microservice architecture relying on AWS SQS. This means even if 10,000 candidates upload resumes simultaneously, the asynchronous queues will process them without crashing our API Gateway."

**Q4: What makes this different from standard ATS tools?**
> "Standard ATS tools just scan for keywords. Zecpath conducts full semantic evaluation, detects live tab-switching cheating, and mathematically flags inconsistent skill profiles."

## The "Architect Stumpers"
**Q5: What happens if a candidate uploads a weirdly formatted resume, like an image?**
> "Thanks to our Day 65 Graceful Degradation patch, the system proactively detects image-based PDFs. Instead of a silent server crash, it returns a polite HTTP 415 error, asking the user to upload a standard text PDF."

**Q6: What if a candidate is brilliant at tech, but terrible at communication? Do they pass?**
> "No, they will be flagged. Our Variance Engine calculates the mathematical standard deviation across all their scores. Extreme inconsistencies trigger a penalty, routing them to human review rather than an automatic hire."

**Q7: How do you defend against legal audits if someone claims the AI unfairly rejected them?**
> "Every single hiring decision generated by Zecpath is logged into a SHA-256 Cryptographic Vault. We maintain an immutable, legally defensible record of exactly which data points led to the decision."
